Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 July 2026

Glint is a menu-bar Git client. It is built to stay out of your way, and that includes your data. In short: Glint collects nothing and sends nothing to us. There are no accounts, no analytics, and no telemetry.

What Glint does not do

  • It does not collect, store, or transmit personal data to us or any third party we control.
  • It has no analytics, tracking, crash reporting, or advertising.
  • It does not require an account and never asks for one.

Data that stays on your device

  • Your repositories. Glint reads and writes your local Git repositories directly on your machine. That data never leaves your device through Glint.
  • Preferences and license. Your chosen theme, language, trial start, and license key are stored locally (in the app's config folder and the OS Keychain). They are not sent to us.

Connections Glint makes on your behalf

  • Git remotes. When you fetch, pull, or push, Glint connects to the Git remote you configured (for example GitHub) using your own SSH keys or credentials. This is the same connection your command-line Git uses.
  • Pull request and CI status. If you have the GitHub CLI signed in, Glint uses your existing token to ask GitHub about the current branch's pull request and checks. The request goes directly from your machine to GitHub; Glint stores nothing and no data passes through us.
  • Updates (direct download build only). The Ko-fi build checks GitHub Releases for a newer version and downloads it. No personal data is sent. The Mac App Store build does not do this; Apple delivers updates.

Purchases

Purchases are handled by Ko-fi or the Mac App Store, each under its own privacy policy. If you buy on Ko-fi, your email is used solely to send you your license key. We do not use it for anything else.
